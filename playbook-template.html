Paratransit Software Sales Playbook — Strategic Interview & Intel-Gathering Plan

Use this playbook to guide sales meetings with agency representatives. Objective: quickly establish credibility, gather decision-driving intel, and position your solution to match operational priorities. Each activity includes who leads it, the goal, sample questions, artifacts to collect, and an estimated time window.

Phase 0 — Preparation (Before first meeting)

  • Lead: Account Executive (AE) + SAE
  • Goal: Arrive informed and with tailored materials
  • Actions:
    • Review public info: fleet size, service area, any recent news.
    • Build a one-page capability map linking your product features to paratransit needs (geocoding, reporting, real-time).
    • Prepare a 2-page agency questionnaire and a demo dataset template.
  • Artifacts: capability map, questionnaire, demo data template
  • Time: 1–2 days

Phase 1 — Discovery Kickoff (Week 1)

  • Lead: AE + Operations SME
  • Goal: Establish relationship, confirm system size and immediate pain points
  • Meeting agenda & sample questions:
    • Quick intro and credibility statement (5–10 min).
    • Confirm system classification:
      • "How many vehicles do you operate daily? Would you classify as small, medium, or large?"
    • High-level KPIs:
      • "What are your typical vehicle miles/day, average rider trip time, and pick-up time variability?"
    • Immediate pain:
      • "What single issue would you fix today if you could?"
    • Decision process:
      • "Who's involved in vendor selection and what's your timeline?"
  • Artifacts to capture: fleet size, KPIs, stakeholders list, immediate hot buttons
  • Time: 60 minutes

Phase 2 — Operational Deep Dive (Week 1–2)

  • Lead: SE + Dispatch/Operations SME (optional follow-up with AE)
  • Goal: Map daily workflows, reservation types, and trip patterns
  • Topics & sample questions:
    • Demand profile:
      • "How many customers/trips per day? How does demand vary by time of day or zone?"
      • "What percent are immediate, advanced, or subscription reservations?"
    • Trip patterns & transfers:
      • "Do you have frequent group trips or many transfers to fixed-route services?"
    • Scheduling effort:
      • "How much manual rework is needed daily for schedules?"
      • "What are your biggest sources of schedule disruption?"
    • Tools & integrations:
      • "What mapping/GIS, accounting, or CAD systems do you use?"
  • Artifacts: trip volume timelines, reservation mix %, list of integrations, sample trip patterns
  • Time: 45–90 minutes

Phase 3 — Technology & Feature Validation (Week 2–3)

  • Lead: SE + Product Manager (if available)
  • Goal: Validate technology requirements, SLAs, and data needs for demos/proof
  • Questions & checks:
    • Real-time needs:
      • "Do you require online pickup estimates and live vehicle tracking? What update frequency?"
    • Database and eligibility:
      • "Do you need automatic population of reservation forms and ADA eligibility checks?"
    • Mapping & geocoding:
      • "How accurate are your current addresses? Do you require geocoded-address verification?"
    • Manual control:
      • "How often do dispatchers override schedules? What must a manual-override UI include?"
    • Reporting & billing:
      • "Which reports are mandatory (e.g., Section 15)? What billing rules must be automated?"
    • User profiles & security:
      • "How many concurrent users? What role-based access is required?"
  • Artifacts: prioritized feature list, SLA targets (e.g., lookup <2s), sample data extract for demo
  • Time: 60–90 minutes

Phase 4 — Stakeholder & Procurement Intelligence (Week 2–4)

  • Lead: AE + Procurement Liaison
  • Goal: Map decision criteria, procurement process, budget cadence, and references
  • Questions & actions:
    • Decision authority:
      • "Who signs off on purchases and who influences the decision?"
    • Procurement timeline:
      • "Is there a formal RFP? When will budget be allocated?"
    • Budget signals:
      • "Is funding available now or is this next fiscal cycle?"
    • Reference expectations:
      • "Would you like references from similar-sized agencies or peer deployments?"
  • Artifacts: decision-maker map, procurement timeline, budget window, evaluation criteria
  • Time: 30–60 minutes

Phase 5 — Field & User Interviews (Week 3–6)

  • Lead: AE + Customer Success Manager (CSM) / Trainer
  • Goal: Gather tactical user needs, resistance points, and training requirements
  • Interview targets & sample questions:
    • Dispatchers/reservationists:
      • "Walk me through a typical reservation from call to confirmation."
      • "Where do you waste the most time? What would reduce it?"
    • Drivers:
      • "How do routing changes reach you? What vehicle info is critical?"
    • Eligibility/compliance staff:
      • "How do you currently validate ADA eligibility and store documentation?"
    • Riders or advocacy groups (optional):
      • "What friction points do riders cite for booking or transfers?"
  • Artifacts: SOP pain points, training needs list, no-show drivers, confirmation needs
  • Time: 30–60 minutes per interview

Phase 6 — Validation Demo & Data Trial Offer (Week 4–8)

  • Lead: SE + AE
  • Goal: Demonstrate fit using their data and collect acceptance criteria
  • Actions:
    • Propose a short trial or sandbox using anonymized sample data.
    • Run scenario-based demos that mirror their top use cases:
      • Immediate reservations, subscription loads, group trip pooling, transfers, Section 15 report generation.
    • Capture performance metrics during demo (response time, accuracy).
  • Artifacts: demo recordings, acceptance checklist, trial data footprint
  • Time: Schedule 1–3 sessions (60–90 min each) over 1–2 weeks

Phase 7 — Competitive & Risk Intelligence (Throughout Weeks 1–8)

  • Lead: AE + Market Intelligence
  • Goal: Identify competing vendors, potential blockers, and compliance gaps
  • Activities & prompts:
    • Ask: "Have you evaluated other vendors? What did you like/dislike?"
    • Probe for internal blockers (IT security, change resistance, data quality).
    • Collect any regulatory constraints (Section 15 specifics, local procurement rules).
  • Artifacts: competitor spreadsheet, blocker mitigation plan, regulatory checklist
  • Time: Ongoing

Phase 8 — Commercial & Implementation Positioning (Week 6–10)

  • Lead: AE + Solutions Architect + CSM
  • Goal: Turn gathered intel into a tailored commercial and implementation proposal
  • Deliverables:
    • Proposal that maps must-have features to your solution and includes pricing tiers tied to fleet size and concurrency.
    • Phased implementation plan: pilot (2–4 weeks), data migration, staff training, cutover checklist.
    • KPI success criteria for 30/60/90-day reviews (vehicle miles/day, pick-up variability, no-shows).
  • Artifacts: tailored proposal, pilot plan, SLA & success KPIs
  • Time: 1–2 weeks to assemble

Phase 9 — Closing Preparation & Reference Reinforcement (Week 8–12)

  • Lead: AE + Customer References + Legal (as needed)
  • Goal: Remove final objections and prepare for procurement
  • Actions:
    • Provide references from agencies of similar size and use cases (Section 15, ADA compliance).
    • Share case studies and pilot outcomes (if trial run).
    • Pre-fill answers for RFP requirements and provide a sample contract with standard SLAs.
  • Artifacts: reference list, RFP response package, draft contract
  • Time: 1–3 weeks depending on procurement cycle

Phase 10 — Win Handoff & Implementation Kickoff (Post-sign)

  • Lead: CSM + Project Manager
  • Goal: Ensure smooth transition and early success
  • Actions:
    • Deliver implementation timeline, training schedule, data migration checklist, and go-live contingency plan.
    • Schedule 30/60/90-day performance reviews and assign a single point of contact for escalations.
  • Artifacts: kickoff pack, success dashboard, support SLAs
  • Time: Immediate post-sign; kickoff within 1–2 weeks

Quick Interview Cheat Sheet (one-page prompts)

  • System size & KPIs: vehicles, vehicle miles/day, avg rider time, pickup variability.
  • Demand mix: trips/day, peak windows, immediate vs advanced vs subscriptions.
  • Pain points: top 3 operational issues; cost/efficiency targets.
  • Must-have tech: geocoding, real-time tracking, manual override, Section 15 reporting, billing automation.
  • Integrations: GIS, accounting, CAD, HR systems; preferred file formats (CSV/XLSX/JSON).
  • Users & security: concurrent users, roles, access restrictions.
  • Procurement: timeline, budget window, decision makers, RFP status.
  • Trial acceptance: success metrics, representative datasets, demo scenario list.

Tactics & Best Practices

  • Lead with empathy & domain credibility: cite quick wins relevant to their system size (e.g., "For 20–40 vehicle agencies we cut no-shows X% with confirmation callbacks").
  • Use their data early: anonymized sample imports speed trust and reveal gaps.
  • Convert pain into measurable success criteria: tie each feature to a KPI you'll track in 30/60/90 days.
  • Address blockers proactively: security questionnaires, Section 15 compliance proof, and reference calls.
  • Timebox conversations: stakeholders are busy — present concise agendas and next steps after each meeting.

Deliverables to Leave After Each Meeting

  • One-page summary of what you learned, key decisions, and next steps (send within 24 hours).
  • Demo/trial request or data template (if agreed).
  • Stakeholder contact map and procurement timeline.
